Interactive Experience Research Response to Rich Brown

Kayleen Nguyen (S4171044) | richbrown.info

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the showreel window in the background zooming as I scrolled through, and the paragraphs of text moving alongside, all without a visible cursor.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Scrolling up to progress through the website
  • Hovering over the list of projects
  • Clicking on different projects to expand them

    • 3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

    I spent most of my time engaging with the list of projects, scrolling through and reading the details of the brands/clients involved, as well as watching the short clips that showcased the results.

    4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

    My most common action in the two-minute interaction of the experience was scrolling through the website.

    5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

    My impression of the intended primary goal was for the designer to showcase their UX and UI design work through a website that can visually demonstrate their skills alongside their past work.

    6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

    The interactive experience communicates this goal thorough using small animations on different elements and using transitions when clicking to different sections of the website, being visually appealing to the user.

    7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

    My impression is that the experience would be interacted with over multiple times, as not only does this website serve as a personal archive of past projects for Brown himself but is also a business portfolio that he can show new clients to look through in their own time, beneficial in the long-term.

    8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

    It communicates how it should be interacted with as an existing portfolio by keeping an extensive archive of Brown’s past projects for anyone to be able to view and possibly get in touch with him if they want to request a similar work for themselves to what he’s done before. People are able to return to the website if necessary.

    9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

    The experience references mobile applications and other portfolio websites, all of which Brown has focused in his role as a UX and UI specialist.

    10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

    These references communicate to me that if I want a more in-depth look at the projects then I would be able to easily access them through Brown’s portfolio website.

    11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

    These references communicate a sense of professionalism that can be expected of Rich Brown, as his extensive experience and way of showcasing it paints him as a reliable and trustworthy designer.

    12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

    The most frustrating part of the interaction was that there was no visible cursor on the website, making it difficult to know where you are currently hovered over until one of the windows move.

    13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

    The most satisfying part of the interaction was the way that the website content would respond dynamically to the direction I scrolled.
