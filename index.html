Interactive Experience Research Response to Rich Brown

Kayleen Nguyen (S4171044) | richbrown.info

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the showreel window in the background zooming as I scrolled through, and the paragraphs of text moving alongside. There was also no visible cursor on the website, which forced me to rely on scrolling down to reach the next area.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Scrolling up to progress through the website
  • Hovering over the list of projects
  • Clicking on different projects to expand them

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of my time engaging with the list of projects, scrolling through and reading the details of the brands/clients involved. I also watched the short clips/GIF attachments included within the descriptions of each project, seeing the processes that were happening to achieve the result.

4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action in the two-minute interaction of the experience was scrolling through the website, as the website relied heavily on that. Apart from that, the other main action to do was clicking on the different elements that redirected me to the other projects that couldn’t be accessed by scrolling alone.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression of the intended primary goal was for the designer to showcase their UX and UI design work through a website that can visually demonstrate their skills and knowledge of website design. The interactive experience also serves as a place for the designer to display their past awards and credentials for people who are interested in what Brown is recognized in.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The interactive experience communicates this goal thorough using smooth transitions during the scrolling process and when users click to go to different sections of the website. The experience also utilises GIF content in each project description, visually appealing to the user.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

My impression is that the experience would be interacted with over multiple times, as not only does this website serve as a personal archive of past projects for Brown himself, but it is also a business portfolio that he can show new clients to look through in their own time, beneficial in the long-term. Other audiences are also able to view this website at their own accord.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

It communicates how it should be interacted with as an existing portfolio by keeping an extensive archive of Brown’s past projects for anyone to be able to view and possibly get in touch with him if they want to request a similar work for themselves to what he’s done before. People are able to return to the website for other purposes.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience references mobile applications and other portfolio websites, all of which Brown has focused in his role as a UX and UI specialist. This provides users with the knowledge that he is an adaptable designer with skills than can transfer to different types of platforms.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These references communicate to me that if I want a more in-depth look at the projects then I would be able to easily access them through Brown’s portfolio website. I would stay on the website for longer to read through his roles in each project and watch the short clips that go through different areas of the project.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

These references communicate a sense of professionalism that can be expected of Rich Brown, as his extensive experience and way of showcasing it paints him as a reliable and trustworthy designer. His own design style is reflected across the website and adapts to the brand of his clients in their own websites that he helps design.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of the interaction was that there was no visible cursor on the website, making it difficult to know where you are currently hovered over until one of the windows move. The animations and GIF content also lagged the website at times, making the overall experience slightly choppy.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction was the way that the website content would respond dynamically to the direction I scrolled. This part was satisfying to me because it was visually appealing, interesting but still remained neat and professional, which would be expected of others across all of his work.
